Data Accuracy & Source Disclaimer

Section 02
Important Notice: The antique identification data, descriptions, historical details, era information, and any other textual content displayed in this App are sourced from publicly available web resources (including Google search results, knowledge bases, and visual search indexes). This data is not verified, certified, or guaranteed to be accurate, complete, or up to date.

Specifically, you should be aware that:

  • Information presented about an antique item (name, origin, era, material, style, provenance) is retrieved via automated AI analysis and may not reflect the exact or true characteristics of the physical item you scanned.
  • The App may occasionally misidentify items, provide partial information, or display data for a similar but different item.
  • Antique identification involves expert judgment, tactile inspection, and provenance verification — none of which the App is capable of performing remotely.
  • Data sourced from the web may contain errors, outdated records, or conflicting historical accounts. We do not independently validate this data.
  • When the App cannot confidently identify an item, it may display a general error or incomplete result rather than fabricated information. If your item is common, modern, or the photo is of poor quality, results may be unreliable.

Use the information provided in the App for educational and general reference purposes only. Do not rely on this data for professional, legal, financial, or commercial decisions.

Estimated Price Disclaimer

Section 03
CRITICAL: Any price range shown in the App is an approximate estimate only and does NOT represent the actual market value, resale value, auction value, or insurance value of your specific item.

Regarding estimated price information displayed in the App:

  • Approximate only: Price ranges are derived from general web data and AI inference, not from real-time auction results, dealer pricing, or expert appraisal databases.
  • Actual value may be lower or higher: The real market value of your item may be significantly lower or significantly higher than the estimated range shown. Many factors affect an item's actual value — condition, provenance, rarity, current market demand, region, and buyer/seller dynamics — none of which the App can fully assess.
  • Not a professional appraisal: The App is not a licensed appraiser. Price estimates should never be used as the basis for buying, selling, insuring, estate planning, or tax purposes.
  • Market fluctuation: Antique values can fluctuate significantly over time. Prices shown are based on data available at the time of analysis and may be outdated.
  • No financial liability: LogicGridApps accepts no liability for any financial loss or gain resulting from actions taken based on the estimated prices shown in the App.

Always consult a certified antique appraiser or specialist dealer before making any financial decisions about your antique items.

Camera & Gallery Data

Section 05

The core feature of the App is identifying vintage items from photographs. To do this, the App requests permission to access your device's Camera and/or local Storage.

How We Process Images:
- When you take a photo or select one from your gallery, the image is cropped and resized locally on your device.
- To identify the item, the image is analyzed using AI models and visual search algorithms. During search operations, images may be temporarily uploaded to a file hosting service (e.g. https://tmpfiles.org) so that visual indexing API calls can inspect them.
- Images uploaded this way are temporary and subject to automated deletion. We do not store or keep images on our own servers, and we do not use your photos to build database profiles of you.

Images you scan are not linked to your identity, are not retained beyond the session analysis period, and are not shared with any party other than the visual analysis pipeline described above.

Data Storage & Retention

Section 09

Your antique collections, custom category folders, and scan history are saved locally on your device's internal SQLite database (via Room Database).

  • Crash reports sent via Firebase Crashlytics are retained by Google for a limited period for debugging purposes only.
